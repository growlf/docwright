# Mission

Cascade STEAM is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Bellingham, WA.

## Statement

*Building a collaborative, supportive, and civic-minded STEAM community for social good.*

## Context

STEAM is an acronym for the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and
Mathematics. STEAM education is a powerful framework to promote curiosity and
creativity, encourage a shared learning experience, and build tools to solve important
life challenges.

Cascade STEAM pursues this mission through two primary modes of activity:

- **Community Groups** — convening aspiring, current, and retired professionals
  interested in STEAM topics to connect, learn, and collaborate
- **Community Projects** — organizing shared learning experiences focused on
  positive community impact, often in collaboration with community partners

All Cascade STEAM programs and events strive to be:
**Free. Open to everyone. Bring your friends.**

## Scope

This mission governs all Cascade STEAM programs, events, decisions, and partnerships.
Any proposal, plan, or work item that cannot be traced back to this mission should be
questioned. Any decision that conflicts with this mission should be declined.

# Values

> **Status: draft** — These values are inferred from the public website, the
> "Free. Open to everyone." operating principle, the community-supported funding
> model, the Code of Conduct, and the open-source community group.
> Leadership should validate, add, or remove values to reflect what is actually

Values are the non-negotiables. When a proposal seems efficient or beneficial but
conflicts with a value, the value wins. A decision record that cites a value
("we declined this because it conflicts with [[values.md#open-access]]") is only
meaningful if the values are real and enforced.

## Open Access

Everything Cascade STEAM does is free, open to everyone, and welcoming to newcomers.
Membership dues, paywalls, or exclusivity requirements are incompatible with the mission.
Programs that require participants to pay, qualify, or belong to a specific group need
explicit policy justification.

*Implication for proposals:* Any proposal that restricts access to Cascade STEAM
programs or events based on ability to pay, prior credentials, or group membership
requires escalation to governance review.

## Community First

Cascade STEAM is community-supported, not grant-dependent or dues-based. Decisions
serve the community, not funders, partners, or organizational self-interest. When
partner or funder interests conflict with community interest, community interest wins.

*Implication for proposals:* Partnerships that require Cascade STEAM to prioritize
partner needs over community access should be declined or restructured.

## Open Source and Open Knowledge

Cascade STEAM builds in the open. Tools we create are open-source. Knowledge we
generate is shared. We are part of the broader open-source and open-knowledge
community, not apart from it.

*Implication for proposals:* Tools, systems, or processes that require proprietary
lock-in, restrict reuse, or create dependency on commercial platforms require explicit
justification and governance review.

## Equity and Inclusion

The "gulf of ignorance" — the gap between those with access to STEAM knowledge and
opportunity and those without — is the problem Cascade STEAM exists to close.
Equity is not a program area; it is the organizing principle of every program area.

*Implication for proposals:* Programs that serve already-advantaged populations
without a path to broader access should be questioned. Diversity in participants,
organizers, and leadership is an operational goal, not a compliance checkbox.

## Civic Responsibility

Cascade STEAM uses STEAM skills for social good, not personal profit. Programs are
oriented toward community benefit. Collaboration with organizations working on civic
challenges is core to the mission.

*Implication for proposals:* Commercial activities that do not clearly serve the
mission require governance review. Consulting and service work should be structured
to build community capacity, not extract it.

## Welcoming Culture

All participants agree to the Cascade STEAM Code of Conduct. The organization strives
to be a place where people of all backgrounds, experience levels, and identities feel
genuinely welcome — not merely tolerated.

*Implication for proposals:* Events, programs, or partnerships that create an
unwelcoming environment for any participant group conflict with this value.

## docwright User Roles

Cascade STEAM uses a four-tier access model in docwright. Role is set via the
`author-role:` frontmatter field on documents created by that contributor.

| Role | Who | What they can do |
|------|-----|-----------------|
| **Observer** | General public, event attendees | Read published policies; submit inbox ideas via web form or email. Cannot edit. |
| **Contributor** | Active community members, students, volunteers | Read all non-restricted content; create issues and proposals; work assigned tasks; ask the AI questions about any document. Cannot promote without steward review. |
| **Steward** | Program leads, co-organizers, experienced contributors | Full read access; promote documents through lifecycle stages; draft policy updates; triage inbox; assign work. AI can act on their behalf with confirmation. |
| **Governance** | Board, officers, senior leadership | Approve policy changes; make final rejection decisions; configure the system. Full AI agency with audit trail. |

## Decision rights by document type

| Document type | Can create | Can promote | Can make final |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Inbox item | Anyone (Observer+) | — | — |
| Issue | Contributor+ | Steward+ | Steward+ |
| Proposal | Contributor+ | Steward+ | Steward+ |
| Plan | Contributor+ | Steward+ | Governance |
| Policy | Steward (draft) | Governance | Governance |
| Decision | Steward (draft) | Governance | Governance |
| Work item | Contributor+ | Steward+ | Steward+ |

## The rejection record requirement

When a proposal is declined, a Decision document is created. It is not a failed
proposal — it is an institutional record of what was considered, what was evaluated,
what was concluded, and why. This record is as important as any accepted proposal.

The AI can surface prior decision records when new similar ideas arrive:
*"We evaluated something like this in [date]. Here's what we found and why we went
a different direction. Still relevant?"*

Losing this institutional memory is how organizations repeat their own mistakes.

# Policy Area: Community Service

## Purpose

Cascade STEAM's community service programs apply STEAM skills directly to community
needs, with a particular focus on digital equity and access for populations who
face barriers to technology participation.

## Current programs in this area

- **Service Corps** — technology training and community service program for social good
- **Collaborative Internship** — extends Service Corps with local organizations for internships
- **Consulting** — business, technology, and community engagement services
- **Digital Navigators** — FREE basic technology support for social service patrons
- **Help Desk** — FREE technology support for the general public by suggested donation
- **Reuse** — refurbishes donated technology equipment for productive community reuse
- **Stormwater Monitoring** — support for RE Sources Water Keeper Program (citizen science)

## Policy principles

- Digital Navigators and Help Desk are core equity programs; funding pressure
  should not convert them to paid services
- Reuse program is both environmental and equity work; devices go to people and
  organizations who need them, not to resale
- Consulting and internship work should build community capacity, not create
  ongoing dependency on Cascade STEAM as a vendor
- Service Corps participants receive training and experience; their work should
  serve the community, not subsidize commercial clients

# Policy Area: STEAM Education

## Purpose

STEAM education is the framework through which Cascade STEAM pursues its mission.
This policy area governs programs and decisions specifically focused on expanding
access to STEAM learning opportunities, career pathways, and skills development —
particularly for populations underrepresented in STEAM fields.

## Scope

This policy area covers:
- Curriculum and workshop development
- School and educational institution partnerships (e.g., Bellingham Public Schools Robotics)
- Mentorship programs connecting students with professionals
- Internship and career pathway programs
- AI and technology literacy workshops

## Policy principles

- STEAM education programs should lower the bar to entry, not raise it
- Programs serving students and early-career participants have priority over
  programs serving established professionals (who have other resources)
- Curriculum and materials developed by Cascade STEAM should be open-licensed
  and available for reuse by other organizations
- Partnerships with schools and educational institutions should be long-term
  commitments, not one-off events

## The "gulf of ignorance"

The concept introduced in Cascade STEAM's design discussions — that the gap between
those with access to STEAM knowledge and those without is the core problem to solve —
is most directly addressed by this policy area. Proposals in this area should be
evaluated against: does this narrow the gulf, widen it, or have no effect?

# Policy Area: Partnerships

## Purpose

Cascade STEAM collaborates with community partners in shared purpose. This policy
area governs how Cascade STEAM identifies, evaluates, and maintains partnerships
with other organizations — including how aligned communities may receive support
to develop their own capacity.

## Partnership types

**Program partners** — organizations that collaborate on specific programs or events
(e.g., RE Sources for Stormwater Monitoring, Bellingham Makerspace for events).

**Support recipients** — organizations that receive Cascade STEAM services
(Digital Navigators, Help Desk, Consulting, Reuse equipment).

**Aligned communities** — organizations with similar vision that Cascade STEAM
identifies, supports, or helps develop. These may eventually adopt docwright
and operate as federated partner vaults.

## Evaluation criteria for new partnerships

A partnership proposal should be able to answer:

1. How does this partnership serve the Cascade STEAM mission?
   See [[policies/core/mission.md]]
2. Does the partner's operating model conflict with Cascade STEAM values?
   See [[policies/core/values.md]] — particularly open access, community-first,
   and open-source principles
3. What does Cascade STEAM give, and what does it receive?
   Partnerships that primarily extract Cascade STEAM resources without
   community benefit require governance review
4. Is this a one-time collaboration or an ongoing relationship?
   Ongoing partnerships need a named steward and a review cadence

## Federation model for aligned communities

When Cascade STEAM identifies a community with aligned vision:

**Fork model (default):** The partner community receives a copy of Cascade STEAM's
policy templates and docwright vault seed to establish their own governance system.
They operate independently. `origin: cascade-steam` frontmatter traces the lineage.

**Federation model (future):** Partner communities may submit Issues/Proposals
into the Cascade STEAM inbox, and vice versa. The AI can cross-reference across
vaults. Requires Phase B of docwright development.

# Proposal: Hardware Workshop Series at Bellingham Public Library

## Problem

Bellingham has a strong STEAM community among professionals, but hands-on hardware
skills — basic electronics, device repair, microcontrollers — are not accessible to
community members without technical backgrounds or expensive tools. The library is a
trusted, accessible, neutral venue that already serves the populations Cascade STEAM
most wants to reach.

## Proposed approach

Run a monthly hardware workshop series at the Bellingham Public Library, free and
open to all ages. Sessions would rotate through topics:
- Basic electronics and soldering
- Device diagnosis and repair (in connection with the Reuse program)
- Introduction to Raspberry Pi / microcontrollers
- Sensor projects and citizen science connections

Equipment would be provided by Cascade STEAM (sourced partly through the Reuse
program's refurbished inventory). Volunteer instructors from the Engineering and
Open Source community groups would lead sessions.

## Alignment with policy

- [[policies/core/mission.md]] — community building through STEAM education ✅
- [[policies/core/values.md#open-access]] — free, open to all ages ✅
- [[policies/core/values.md#equity-and-inclusion]] — library venue reaches
  populations without existing tech access ✅
- [[policies/program-areas/community-building.md]] — new community building program ✅
- Possible connection to [[policies/program-areas/community-service.md]] via
  Reuse program equipment sourcing

## Risks and open questions

- Venue availability: requires coordination with Bellingham Public Library
- Equipment storage between sessions: library may not have storage capacity
- Volunteer instructor consistency: monthly cadence requires committed organizers
- Budget: consumable electronics costs; needs an estimate

## Recommendation

Proceed to planning if a steward is willing to own the library relationship.
This proposal is ready for promotion to Plan status pending steward assignment.
